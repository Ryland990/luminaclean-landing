Best Duplicate Photo Cleaner Apps for iPhone in 2026

Your iPhone camera gets better every year, which means your photo library grows faster every year. And with it comes the inevitable buildup of duplicates, near-identical shots, blurry images, and forgotten screenshots quietly eating your storage.

Apple introduced a Duplicates album in iOS 16 that can merge exact copies, and it is a useful starting point. But it has a significant limitation: it only catches pixel-identical images. If you took five shots of the same sunset, each slightly different, the Duplicates album will not flag any of them. It also will not identify blurry photos, old screenshots, or large videos you no longer need.

That is where a dedicated duplicate photo cleaner app comes in. These apps use AI and visual recognition to find not just exact copies, but similar photos, blurry shots, and other clutter that Apple's built-in tools miss. Some newer apps are also introducing features that no built-in tool offers — like the ability to filter and scan your library by year, so you can clean up one era at a time instead of facing thousands of photos at once.

We tested the most popular photo cleaner apps for iPhone available in early 2026, evaluating them on duplicate detection accuracy, how well they find similar (not just identical) photos, ease of use, privacy practices, pricing fairness, and extra features like video compression. Here are our top five picks.

Key Takeaways

  • Apple's built-in Duplicates album only catches exact copies — dedicated apps find similar photos, blurry shots, screenshots, and large videos too.
  • All five apps on this list process photos on-device, meaning your images never leave your phone.
  • Clever Cleaner is the best completely free option with no paywalls or ads. LuminaClean offers the most features with a generous free tier.
  • Subscription costs vary widely — from completely free to nearly $260/year. Check pricing carefully before committing.
  • A thorough first cleanup typically frees 2–5 GB. Users with large libraries often recover 5–10 GB or more.

How We Tested

We installed each app on an iPhone running iOS 18 with a library of approximately 9,500 photos accumulated over three years. This library included intentional duplicates (saved from messaging apps), burst sequences, similar shots from travel, blurry images, and over 600 screenshots. We evaluated each app on how many items it correctly identified, false positive rate, scanning speed, interface quality, and how transparent the pricing model was.

2. Clever Cleaner — Best Free Option

2 Clever Cleaner by CleverFiles

Clever Cleaner is a standout because it is genuinely, completely free — no paywalls, no ads, no hidden subscriptions. Developed by CleverFiles (the team behind Disk Drill), it uses AI to detect both exact duplicates and similar photos, and it does so surprisingly well for a free app.

In our testing, Clever Cleaner scanned our 9,500-photo library in about two minutes and caught the majority of duplicates we expected. Its Smart Cleanup mode automatically selects the best photo from each group and marks others for deletion, which speeds up the review process considerably. The app also handles screenshots, Live Photo compression, and large file detection.

The interface is clean and color-coded, with separate tabs for Similars, Heavies, Screenshots, and Lives. The swipe-based review mode works well for quick decisions. All deleted photos go to a Trash folder within the app first, giving you an extra safety net beyond iOS's Recently Deleted album.

Where Clever Cleaner falls short compared to LuminaClean is in extra features — there is no way to filter or scan by year (a feature unique to LuminaClean), no adjustable sensitivity sliders, and no duplicate contact merging. The similar photo detection, while good, occasionally missed some near-identical shots that LuminaClean caught. But for a completely free app, the value is exceptional.

Which App Should You Choose?

  • Best completely free option: Clever Cleaner — no paywalls, no ads, genuinely free with good AI detection.

For the best results, we recommend a two-step approach: start with Apple's built-in Duplicates album (Photos > Albums > Utilities > Duplicates) to merge exact copies, then use a dedicated app to catch the similar photos, blurry shots, screenshots, and large videos that the built-in tool overlooks.

A Note on Pricing Transparency

One thing we noticed during testing is that pricing in this category varies enormously and is not always transparent. Some apps advertise as "free" but lock all meaningful features behind subscriptions that can range from a few dollars per month to over $5 per week (which adds up to over $260 per year). Others use dark patterns to make free trials auto-renew.

Before downloading any photo cleaner, we recommend checking the App Store listing carefully, reading the "In-App Purchases" section, and being cautious with any app that asks you to start a free trial before you have even used it. The apps on this list were selected partly because they are upfront about their pricing.
