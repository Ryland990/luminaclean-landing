How to Delete Duplicate Photos on iPhone

If you have ever scrolled through your iPhone's photo library and noticed the same image appearing two, three, or even four times, you are not alone. Duplicate photos are one of the most common reasons iPhones run out of storage. Whether they come from saving images from messaging apps, syncing issues with iCloud, or simply snapping multiple shots of the same scene, duplicates quietly eat away at your available space.

In this guide, we will walk through three proven methods to delete duplicate photos on iPhone — from doing it by hand to using smart tools that automate the entire process. By the end, you will know exactly which approach fits your needs.

Why Do Duplicate Photos Pile Up?

Before diving into solutions, it helps to understand how duplicates accumulate in the first place. Common causes include:

  • Messaging apps — Saving the same photo from Messages, WhatsApp, or email multiple times.
  • iCloud sync conflicts — Turning iCloud Photos on and off, or syncing across multiple devices, can create duplicates.
  • Editing workflows — Some editing apps save a copy alongside the original.
  • AirDrop and file transfers — Receiving a photo you already have creates an identical copy.
  • Burst mode and Live Photos — These can create near-identical images that clutter your library.

Over months and years, these duplicates compound. It is not unusual for users to discover hundreds — even thousands — of duplicate or near-identical images taking up gigabytes of storage.

Key Takeaways

  • Duplicate photos come from messaging apps, iCloud sync, editing workflows, AirDrop, and burst mode.
  • iOS 16+ has a built-in Duplicates album, but it only catches exact copies -- not similar photos.
  • Dedicated apps like LuminaClean use AI to find duplicates, similar shots, blurry images, and screenshots.
  • A thorough cleanup can free several gigabytes of iPhone storage in minutes.

Method 1: Manually Finding and Deleting Duplicates

The most straightforward approach is to scroll through your Photos app and delete duplicates one by one. Here is how:

  1. Open the Photos app on your iPhone.
  2. Go to the Library tab and switch to All Photos view.
  3. Scroll through your images, looking for repeated photos.
  4. Tap Select in the top right corner, then tap each duplicate you want to remove.
  5. Tap the trash icon to delete the selected images.
  6. Go to Albums > Recently Deleted and tap Delete All to permanently free up space.

Pros

  • No extra app needed
  • Full control over what gets deleted
  • Works on any iOS version

Cons

  • Extremely time-consuming
  • Easy to miss duplicates
  • Impractical for large libraries

If you have a small photo library with just a few hundred images, manual deletion can work. But for most people with thousands of photos, this method is simply not feasible.

Method 2: Using the Built-In iOS Duplicates Album (iOS 16+)

With iOS 16, Apple introduced a dedicated Duplicates album inside the Photos app. This feature automatically scans your library and groups exact duplicate images together, letting you merge them with a single tap.

How to Use the iOS Duplicates Album

  1. Make sure your iPhone is running iOS 16 or later (check in Settings > General > About).
  2. Open the Photos app and go to the Albums tab.
  3. Scroll down to the Utilities section.
  4. Tap Duplicates.
  5. You will see groups of duplicate photos. Tap Merge next to any pair to keep the highest-quality version and delete the rest.
  6. To merge all at once, tap Select, then Select All, and tap Merge.

Pros

  • Built directly into iOS
  • No downloads required
  • Keeps the best quality version

Cons

  • Only detects exact duplicates
  • Misses similar (not identical) photos
  • Requires iOS 16 or later
  • Album can take days to populate

Apple's built-in tool is a great first step, but it has a significant limitation: it only catches exact duplicates. If you took three nearly identical photos of the same sunset — each slightly different — the Duplicates album will not flag them. You also will not find blurry photos, old screenshots, or other clutter that is wasting your storage.

Tips to Prevent Future Duplicates

Once you have cleaned up your library, here are a few habits that will help keep duplicates from piling up again:

  • Avoid saving photos multiple times from messaging apps.
  • Keep iCloud Photos turned on consistently across all your devices to prevent sync conflicts.
  • Review burst shots right after taking them and keep only the best one.
